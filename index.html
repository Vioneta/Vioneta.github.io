Use Cases

The Vioneta Smart Agriculture Platform combines edge AI, computer vision, interoperable sensors (LoRa/RS485/IP), and automation to address diverse production systems – all operating reliably online or offline.

Vineyards & Orchards

  • Microclimate & leaf wetness monitoring
  • AI‑driven irrigation optimization
  • Vision‑based yield & ripeness insights

Arable Crops & Vegetables

  • Soil health & nutrient monitoring
  • Automated irrigation & fertigation
  • Predictive planting & harvest planning

Greenhouse Management

  • AI climate control (temp, humidity, CO₂)
  • Smart lighting & energy optimization
  • Precision fertigation & stress detection

Poultry Farms

  • AI wellness analytics from vocalization & behavior
  • Computer‑vision weight estimation
  • Env. optimization and automated egg counting

Pig Production

  • Digital log book for operations & health
  • Optimize temperature, humidity, CO₂/NH₃
  • AI‑assisted decisions and early alerts

Asset Monitoring

  • RFID‑based identity for tools and containers
  • Gate readers track flow & locations
  • Process compliance and loss prevention

Impact by Domain

Poultry

  • 2–4× faster issue detection via wellness analytics
  • 5–10% improvement in FCR/mortality
  • 15–30% labor hours saved (automated monitoring)
  • 10–20% energy savings (ventilation/lighting optimization)

Pig Production

  • 3× faster compliance & record keeping (digital logbook)
  • 8–12% feed/cost savings from environmental control
  • 20–35% labor reduction (automated routines & alerts)
  • 10–18% energy savings (HVAC optimization)

Greenhouse

  • 10–25% energy reduction (lighting, fans, heating)
  • 2–3× faster stress detection (vision + sensors)
  • 15–30% labor hours saved (automated schedules)
  • 10–25% resource savings (fertilizer/chemicals via precision)

Crops & Orchards

  • 2–3× faster scouting & harvest planning (CV insights)
  • 12–25% labor savings (automated irrigation/fertigation)
  • 6–12% energy savings (pumping & scheduling)
  • 10–25% input reduction (nutrients/chemicals, precision)

Ranges are indicative and vary by farm size, baseline practices, climate, crop/livestock type, and system configuration. Actual results depend on deployment scope and data quality.
